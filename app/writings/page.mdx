Longer-form explorations of quality theory, its philosophical foundations, and practical applications.

## Foundations

What qualities are and how they sit at the center of metaphysics, science, and inquiry.

**[Two Definitions of Quality](/writings/two-definitions-of-quality)** — The word "quality" has two distinct but interconnected meanings that shape how we perceive and evaluate the world. This foundational article explores quality as both characteristic and excellence, revealing the deep connection between perception and evaluation.

**[Expanding the Realm of Science to Include Good](/writings/expanding-the-realm-of-science-to-include-good)** — Just as consciousness has entered scientific inquiry, Quality (or *good*) must be recognized as foundational to science itself. This article explores how Quality is not peripheral but essential to scientific reasoning, and how expanding our conception of truth to include harmony and benefit can transform scientific ontology.

## Dynamics of Quality

How qualities are selected, prioritized, and directed—how value emerges within a field of distinctions.

**[Motives and the Direction of Quality](/writings/motives-direction-of-quality)** — Motives as the orienting counterpart to qualities: how evaluative weight arises, and whether direction in a field of qualities requires consciousness.
